#### Hal on his Novice Program

The following 5K novice training program lasts eight weeks and climaxes at the end of Week 8 with a 5K (3.1 mile) race. It is among the easiest programs I offer both online and in books. If you are a beginning runner, just about to take your first running step, this is the program for you.

‚ÄúDo you need to race to call yourself a runner?‚Äù I ask in my book, _RunFast_. Not really. Nevertheless, nailing a fast time in a 5K and comparing it a few months or years later to a time previously run can be an important motivational tool.

5K Novice assumes that you have no major health problems, are in reasonably good shape, and have done at least some jogging or walking. If running 1.5 miles for your first workout in Week 1 seems too difficult, you might want to begin with my [Beginning Runner‚Äôs Guide](https://www.halhigdon.com/training-programs/other/beginning-runners-guide/). If 5K Novice seems too _easy_, consider [5K intermediate](https://www.halhigdon.com/training-programs/5k-training/intermediate-5k/) or even [5K advanced](https://www.halhigdon.com/training-programs/5k-training/advanced-5k/).

The terms used in the training schedule are somewhat obvious, but let me explain what I mean anyway.

**Rest:** Rest days are as vital as training days. They give your muscles time to recover so you can run again. Actually, your muscles will build in strength as you rest.

**Run:** Don‚Äôt worry about how fast you run; just cover the distance‚Äìor approximately the distance suggested. Ideally, you should be able to run at a pace that allows you to converse comfortably while you do so.

**Run/Walk:** There‚Äôs nothing in the rules that suggests you have to run continuously, either in training or in the 5K race itself. Run until fatigued; walk until recovered.

**Walk:** Don‚Äôt worry about how fast you walk, or how much distance you cover. Begin with about 30 minutes and add 5 minutes a week until you peak with a full hour in Week 7.

The following schedule is only a guide. Feel free to make minor modifications to suit your work and family schedule. You will find more information on 5K training in my book, _RunFast_.
